All She Never Wanted

Ragana62

Summary:

As Minister for Magic, Kingsley Shacklebolt knew he was capable of a great many things. Those things did not however, seem to include successfully getting over his lingering affections for a particular young witch who was unfortunately already attached to a thoroughly undeserving Ronald Weasley. When the Wizengamot shoves through a Marriage Law, will he finally get his chance to show Hermione how he feels? She may never have seen this coming, but Hermione Granger has never been one to shy away from a challenge, especially one as thrilling as this.

Chapter 1: And So it Begins

Chapter Text

As Minister for Magic, Kingsley Shacklebolt knew he was capable of a great many things. Sure, given he had been born the heir to one of the oldest and most respected pureblood families in Britain, obtained a more than respectable seven NEWTs, then spent years of his professional life as Head Auror, this was not exactly new information. However, it was relevant to his current predicament. Because, while ensuring that Death Eaters and their sympathizers within the Ministry and Wizengamot were brought to justice in the aftermath of the Second Wizarding War and getting elected to a full term as Minister despite running on a platform of major reform initiatives that even just three years ago were seen as scandalously radical were clearly perfectly within his abilities, recent events had made clear that there were two things he was not capable of.

Turning off the tap and drying his face, he looked himself square in the eyes, interrogating himself in the mirror as he used to any number of Dark Wizards. In fact, he might prefer the Dark Wizards at the moment, at least they had the decency to be relatively easy to sort out at the end of the day. But as he stood in the lavishly decorated private bathroom adjacent to the Minister’s office staring himself down, he knew this wouldn’t be half so simple as a well worded threat or offer of leniency in sentencing for cooperation to get the desired answers from. There was nothing for it now, and it would not do to break the calm, collected manner he was well-known for maintaining in a crisis. At least not until he was in the safety and privacy of his own home, because Kingsley Shacklebolt was staring down the business end of the wand of a reality that was quickly shaping up to be far more terrifying than anything he had faced in all his years as an Auror.

He quickly left the bathroom, stopping only briefly to sweep the two memos on his desk that had started this whole predicament into his briefcase, before stepping into the familiar warmth of the green flames of the floo fire. There really wasn’t anything he could do about it at this point after all, and a good run followed by a calming cup of herbal tea would do wonders for setting him to rights before tomorrow brought all his anxieties to his proverbial and literal doorstep. After all, he was quite used to maintaining a degree of control, even in a crisis, and tomorrow was shaping up to be a crisis fueled by the two things in his life he was quickly coming to terms with his utter inability to have any influence over whatsoever. The first, that blasted marriage law the Wizengamot saw fit to institute in hopes of rectifying the joint problems of falling birth rates and rising frustrations with the wizarding establishment in the post-war period. But he had been given time to come to terms with that one; it had been all anyone seemed to want to talk about in the past month since it had been announced. Even before then, some in his social circles had been debating it for months prior while the court deliberated it before shoving it through by such a majority that his signature hadn’t even been necessary to institute it.

The second, and far more daunting, of the two crises was looking up at him from the table where it had fallen out of his briefcase, practically mocking him really. Being Minister did have its advantages after all, and one of those presently was knowing that the young witch who had caught his eye years ago now, though he would never have admitted to it under normal circumstances, would be greeted at breakfast tomorrow with a letter informing her that she would be expected to marry him, whether she liked it or not, at threat of losing her wand and being dragged off to Azkaban for non-compliance. And, Kingsley thought morosely, like it, she likely would not. He turned away quickly; it would not do to dwell on the inevitable. No, a quick run through his favorite nearby parks, followed by the mint tea he was becoming rather partial to and an early bed, would be just the thing.

The Burrow was bustling with activity. This on its own was hardly noteworthy, as Saturday brunch had become a regular occurrence in the years since the war, but given this week had the full family in attendance and before 9 AM at that, it was downright spooky. So, Hermione thought as she looked around the table at her de facto surrogate family in the Wizarding World, was the reason for it. At least, she noted, she wasn’t the only one who seemed nervous about the contents of the letters that were expected any minute now. While Harry and Ginny were safely engaged 2 months ahead of the passage of the law and were thus excused from it, in no small part thanks to a helpful warning from Kingsley that the Wizengamot was getting closer to making the law a reality, the way that Percy was carefully avoiding eye contact with any of his siblings or parents was a solid sign that he was at least as nervous as she was about what was coming.

 It wasn’t that she was unhappy, not so much so anyways that it would really be the end of the world if she was paired with Ron like he and his mother seemed certain would occur, she just couldn’t share their certainty. Everything she had read about the law seemed to suggest that the Department of Mysteries had used every available means to ensure that pairings were of the highest compatibility possible, and something about the volatile relationship she had shared with Ron since their kiss at the Battle of Hogwarts just didn’t scream “soulmates” to her. Not that she would ever say that out loud of course, not with so much of her life caught up in her Wizarding family after her own parents were still unable to be located yet and Harry’s engagement with Ginny, but the doubt had been weighing on her since the law was announced. At least, she thought, one way or another, I’ll know soon. Either he’s my match, and I can put all this to rest, or he isn’t, and I’ll have an excuse to end things amicably.

Before she could give it too much more thought though a shout rang out. Molly must have noticed the owls approaching through the kitchen window, because by the time she brought over the last plate of pancakes, the window by the table had been opened and the Ministry owls had dropped the anticipated letters in the center of the table where they were being stared at with a mix of concern and excitement by the gathered group. Excited squeals went up from Ginny when she and Harry quickly tore theirs open, confirming that they had managed to get in before the deadline and would simply be expected to marry before the end of the year or be reassigned. Hermione was happy for them, really, it would be hard not to be seeing a close friend and the man who was her brother in all ways than blood be so happy, but with the impending threat of her own letter hanging over her, she was fairly certain that offering them any more than a small smile would leave her at risk of losing the bit of breakfast she had already eaten. Ron, for his part, was more interested in the breakfast on his plate. 

“It’s not like we don’t already know what they say right?”, he said with a mouth full of pancakes as he smiled at Hermione. 

Well, that settled it, she officially was feeling nauseous.

Molly, for her part, was smiling happily between them before looking over at her other sons. 

“Boys, why don’t you go ahead and open yours?” 

Charlie and Bill both sat back, arching eyebrows at their remaining siblings. Charlie living abroad had managed to spare him from the law, the lucky bastard, and with Bill and Fleur still happily married, he didn’t have anything to worry about either. Percy looked about as sick as Hermione felt, so it was George who broke the awkward silence by tearing into his before looking around and loudly announcing “Hermione! Looks like you’re getting an upgrade after all!” 

Immediately, Ron practically dived over the table, lunging for the letter while turning nearly as red as his hair, sputtering, while Molly looked nervously between them. 

“George dear, are you quite sure you opened your own letter?”

 She reached for the letter herself, only for George to laugh and offer “just kidding, it’s Angie, no shock there. But if you ever get bored of my git of a brother, you know where to find me.”, winking dramatically at Hermione as Ron was pulled back into his seat by Charlie, angrily shoveling another piece of bacon into his mouth.

 “Not funny mate.”

Arthur looked between his sons, “Boys, we are all a little stressed at the moment. Perhaps it is best to keep the joking to a minimum, and just open the letters, hmm?” 

Amidst the chaos, Percy had slipped open his letter, neatly unfolding it, reading it over closely, as his father looked over to him. 

“Percy, why don’t you go ahead, it seems you’ve already had time to read yours over?” 

Turning slightly more pale than usual, Percy muttered something a little too quiet to hear.

 “What was that dear?” Molly looked at him eagerly. 

Percy sat up a little straighter, taking a moment to compose himself as he looked around the table, a determination setting into his eyes as he clearly announced “Oliver Wood.” 

“Wood? But he’s a bloke!” Ron shouted, looking between Percy and his own envelope with a horrified look in his eyes, “They can’t do that can they?” 

He dove for his envelope as George let out a shout of his own, “Ha! I knew it! There was no way that you two went at it like that about practice schedules or the appropriate use of the common areas for studying or whatever without, you know, going at it!”

 Ginny let out a squeal like when she opened her own letter before shouting “Congrats brother mine, now you’ll have to let me drag you out to matches, he and Angie are on the same team!”

Hermione gave Percy a small smile as he blushed under the positive attention he was receiving from George and Ginny as Ron looked warily at his own envelope as he read it over a second time. Molly quickly schooled the shocked look from her own face, turning attention to Ron and Hermione rather than dealing with her own thoughts about her recently returned son and his new intended spouse. “Ron dear, you look concerned, they didn’t make a mistake with your assignment as well, did they?”

Ron looked up, briefly meeting Hermione’s eye. This was the moment of truth, she thought, picking at her pancakes. If this was going to be her last Weasley Saturday Brunch, she may as well enjoy it before getting shouted out of the house by an offended Molly. Ron looked between her and his mother, looking slightly nervous. 

“I got Romilda Vane!” 

Now it was Harry’s turn to look a bit green, “you mean that crazy stalker who spent all of sixth year trying to slip me a love potion?” 

Well, at least if it was all going south, she’d get a good laugh out of it first, Hermione thought, taking a sip of her tea. George meanwhile seemed to think that the funniest thing he had heard in a long time and was quickly joined by Bill and Charlie as they burst out laughing as Harry looked horrified between Ron and Hermione. 

“I mean yeah, clearly, they messed it up. I mean she’s got a nice rack and all, but like clearly, it’s supposed to be Mione, right?”

 Ron looked up at her expectantly while the rest of his siblings, Percy and Ginny included after Ron’s latest contribution to the conversation, continued laughing.

Harry looked nervously at her as the laughter died down, but it was Ginny who beat him to the question that clearly everyone was thinking. “Go on then Hermione, we may as well send one letter asking them to explain themselves, who’d you get?” 

Before she could reply, Harry jumped in “I’ve got a galleon on McLaggen” 

“I’ll take that action on Zabini!” George butted in, as the table quickly descended into laughter again, with Fleur quickly asking if Viktor was planning to fly in to dramatically declare his renewed affections and spare her the suffering of an inopportune match, before Bill managed to regain order at the table long enough to solemnly declare his own thoughts on the matter.

“This isn’t funny, this is a good friend’s future happiness we are discussing, and everything I’ve seen about methodology suggests that they are taking compatibility very seriously. Obviously, the smart money is on Snape.” 

At the mere mention of the sour-faced potion master who if possible had become only more reclusive after narrowly surviving his injuries at the end of the war being the name in her envelope the table quickly devolved into chaos, while Hermione’s wide eyes quickly began scanning her letter.

Miss Hermione Jean Granger, OM1,

On behalf of the Ministry of Magic and our newly formed Department of Wizarding Life, we congratulate you on your upcoming nuptials. On the attached page you will find the name, photo, and some basic information pertaining to your intended. We assure you that every precaution was taken to ensure only the most successful matches were made, with every attention given to maximizing the potential compatibility of each assigned couple across multiple categories. Per compatibility assessments undertaken as a joint effort between the Department of Wizarding Life and the Department of Mysteries (Love Room Division), we are pleased to inform you that your match was one of our highest reported compatibility scores! We wish you and your intended every happiness as you embark upon this new chapter of your lives together. Please plan to make contact with your match as soon as possible, as we remind you that all pairs are expected to marry by the end of the calendar year unless otherwise specified in this letter, or additional extensions are applied for and granted in cases of medical necessity, finishing education, or select emergency situations. Review the below information as well as the attached page pertaining to your intended as thoroughly as possible to ensure no misunderstandings occur. You may communicate any questions or concerns to the Department of Wizarding Life, along with confirmation of your wedding date to schedule an officiant. Please note that all bondings must be performed by a licensed officiant approved by the Ministry of Magic and are required to include, at a minimum, standard fidelity bindings unless otherwise specified below. Thank you for your cooperation, and much happiness to you and your intended!

Compatibility Results: 96%

Deadline for Bonding Rites to be Completed: September 31st, 2008*

Bonding Requirements: Advanced Fidelity Bonds*

Amanda Hodges, Head of Department of Wizarding Life

Well, that was certainly something, she thought, though the advanced bonds and expedited date would require further thought. Turning the page, she continued reading.

Please note that your match has been marked for an advance deadline due to the following circumstances: Member Status

Well that at least answered that question, the Ministry intended to make an example out of her. It wasn’t anything new, since the war there had been several ministry events where the Golden Trio as they were now dubbed had been trotted out like show dogs in support of whatever cause or new policy. While she generally didn’t have anything against Kingsley’s policies and had no issue appearing on behalf of various charities whether it was that werewolf advocacy group that Lavender had helped start following the attack from Greyback or the benefit for War Orphans that Harry immediately RSVPed yes to on behalf of all of them when it arrived with his morning post, there was a difference between being asked to appear in support of new legislation aimed at increasing inclusion in Wizarding Britain and being asked to throw the equivalent of a Royal Wedding with some random stranger. Continuing down the sheet, she spotted the technical answer to her second question, though if anything it only opened more questions than answers.

Please note that your match has been marked for altered bonding requirements due to the following reasons: Compatibility Score, Member Obligations

These additional requirements may be discussed with the Department of Wizarding Life upon request when applying for an officiant on your desired date. Please plan at least 30 minutes for this meeting in order to ensure your complete understanding of adjusted bond requirements. 

What obligations Hermione had that required whatever “Advanced Fidelity Bonds” meant she couldn’t say, but at least she knew where to start her research. As she continued to read, Hermione realized that the table had fallen silent again. Looking up, she realized that all eyes are carefully trained on her as she continued to read her letter. 

Breaking the silence, Harry reached over to squeeze her hand that she hadn’t realized had begun to shake a little as she looked down at the final page in her letter. “Who’d you get, Hermione? Honestly, it can’t be that terrible, can it? It’s not like they’d give you Malfoy or whatever, right?” Harry looked over at her earnestly, before his eyes widened further. “It’s not Malfoy, is it? I know they’re reformed or whatever now, but I can’t have a ferret for a brother-in-law Hermione, I just can’t!” 

He smiled wider, clearly trying to put her at ease, though clearly the longer she remained silent, the more he grew nervous. It wasn’t his fault really, he was doing exactly what he should be to try and offer her comfort, the problem is that it wasn’t Malfoy, or McLaggen, or even Ron. Those names she would know how to feel about. But as she sets her letter down slowly, looking up from the softly smiling face of her soon to be husband to meet the waiting gaze of her surrogate family, Hermione realized that this uncertainty was far worse than a simple bad match she could contest, or a perfectly serviceable relationship that while she didn’t feel was perfect at least offered a degree of comfort in its familiarity.

“It’s Kingsley.”
